Chicken Cordon Bleu

Description

Chicken Cordon Bleu is a classic and popular dish in Western cuisine, particularly in American and European culinary traditions. It's a delicious, rich dish that combines chicken, cheese, and ham.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 4 slices of ham
  • 4 slices of Swiss cheese (Gruyère or other melting cheeses can also be used)
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (Panko breadcrumbs work well for extra crispiness)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: Additional herbs or spices like paprika, garlic powder, or thyme for seasoning
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil or butter (for frying, if you choose to fry)
  • Toothpicks (for securing the chicken rolls)

Instructions

  1. Pound 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts to 1/4 inch thickness between sheets of plastic wrap or in a ziplock bag.
  2. Season both sides of each chicken breast with salt, pepper, and any additional herbs or spices you prefer.
  3. Place a slice of Swiss cheese and then a slice of ham on each breast.
  4. Roll up each breast tightly and secure the ends with toothpicks.
  5. Set up three shallow dishes: one with 1/2 cup all-purpose flour, one with 2 beaten eggs, and one with 1 cup breadcrumbs.
  6. Dredge each chicken roll in flour, dip in beaten egg, and coat with breadcrumbs.
  7. To Fry and Bake: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil or butter in a skillet over medium-high heat. Fry chicken rolls until golden brown on all sides, then transfer to a baking dish and bake for 20-30 minutes, or until cooked through.
  8. To Bake Only: Place breaded rolls on a greased baking sheet and bake in preheated oven for 30-40 minutes or until golden and cooked through.
  9. Remove toothpicks, let the chicken rest for a few minutes, and serve. Optionally, serve with a sauce of your choice.
